## Cross-Cutting Issues (raised by multiple reviewers)

### Conceptual consistency and alignment across chapters
- [ ] **Align terminology and typologies across chapters** — Different chapters use inconsistent terms for the same concepts (e.g., "numerical reproduction" vs "computational reproduction", "recreate reproduction" vs "recoding reproduction"). Table 4.1, Figure 4.1, Figure 4.2, Sections 2.3, 2.4, and Chapter 7 all present overlapping but inconsistent typologies of reproduction/replication types and goals. Streamline and reconcile these throughout. (R1, R2, R3)
- [ ] **Ensure chapters relate to each other more clearly** — Sections feel siloed; add cross-references and ensure consistent framing across chapters written by different contributors. (R1, R2, R3)
- [ ] **Link back to deductive vs inductive framework** — The deductive/inductive perspective on replication success (Section 2) is praised but underutilised. Connect it to: Section 6.3 (Changes in Methods), Section 7.2 (Interpreting Divergent Results), Figure 2.1, and the discussion of hidden moderators. (R1, R3)
- [ ] **Clarify the target audience** — Is the handbook for researchers new to empirical research generally, or specifically for those new to replication/reproduction? This affects depth and scope of many sections. (R2, R3)
- [ ] **Add constraints on generality discussion** — Discuss Simons et al. (2017) CoG framework more substantively, linking to WEIRD sampling issues and contextual sensitivity (Section 2.3). Currently only briefly mentioned in Section 6.3. (R1, R2)
- [ ] **Expand discussion of preregistration consistently** — Clarify whether referring to preregistering the reproduction/replication itself or targeting preregistered studies. Treat deviations from preregistration consistently across Chapters 5 and 6. (R1, R2)
- [ ] **Expand use of real-life examples** — The handbook is strongest when discussing actual replication cases. Add more practical, worked examples throughout. (R1, R3)
- [ ] **Use "this handbook" consistently** — Currently alternates between "paper", "article", and "handbook", which may confuse readers. (R2)

### Content gaps to address
- [ ] **Balance reproduction coverage** — Reproduction gets far less space than replication (essentially two sentences in Section 2). Move Table 4.1 classification earlier and present reproduction subcategories (numerical vs robustness) more prominently. (R2)
- [ ] **Add parallel sections for reproduction in Chapter 7** — Currently missing: Defining Reproduction Success, Interpreting Reproduction Failures. (R3)
- [ ] **Expand data integrity checking** — Even without raw data, partial checks are possible beyond statcheck/papercheck. This "reproduction light" approach deserves more than five lines. Reference Heathers (2025) in this context too, not just for study selection. (R3)
- [ ] **Add hands-on practical guidance** — Address practical decisions like the "choice vs strategy" problem (e.g., should a replicator include the same covariates or apply the same selection rule?). Explain how to actually use tools like Altmetric rather than just name-dropping them. (R3)
- [ ] **Add discussion of data standardisation / FAIR principles** — FAIR-ification addresses both practical and scientific implications of data planning, acquisition, and dissemination. It also solves problems related to human-in-the-loop issues (Section 5). (R2)
- [ ] **Add discussion of incentives and career implications** — Current treatment (Koole & Lakens, 2012, in one table cell) is insufficient. Expand on career risks and benefits of replication work, especially for ECRs. (R2)
- [ ] **Balance the MRI deep dive** — Section 9 goes much deeper than other topics. Either pare it back to match the rest of the book, or add comparable depth for another field. Also add a section on identifying field-specific challenges for any discipline. (R1)
- [ ] **Discuss what "the same" means in replication** — The ambiguity of "doing the same" (same choices vs same decision strategy) is a central practical dilemma. Address the "choice vs strategy" problem explicitly. (R3)
- [ ] **Add "checking adherence to preregistration" discussion** — Currently placed in reproduction (p. 27) but unclear how it fits there. Move or justify. (R3)
- [ ] **Explore counter-narratives for mixed results** — When original paper reports a mix of significant and non-significant results across studies, discuss how to interpret this (integrous reporting? context dependence? same direction but varying significance vs opposing directions?). (R1)
